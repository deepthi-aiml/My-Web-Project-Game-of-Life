Game Rules

  • Any live cell with fewer than 2 live neighbors dies (underpopulation)
  • Any live cell with 2 or 3 live neighbors lives on
  • Any live cell with more than 3 live neighbors dies (overpopulation)
  • Any dead cell with exactly 3 live neighbors becomes alive (reproduction)

About Conway's Game of Life

The Game of Life is a cellular automaton devised by mathematician John Conway. It's a zero-player game that evolves based on its initial state. Despite its simple rules, it can produce incredibly complex and fascinating patterns that resemble living organisms.
